Field_Note_002 — Public_LogicLaunch Day 002  Sep 1, 2025

Public_Logic didn’t start in a boardroom. It started in my life — rebuilding from health setbacks, career uncertainty, and the kind of gray every municipal employee knows too well. Gray processes. Gray accountability. Gray systems that don’t connect.

This is our launch day. Public_Logic isn’t about selling another municipal app. It’s about giving staff, boards, and residents the clarity they need to succeed. We’re practitioner-builders: we know what it feels like to be on the inside of a Select Board meeting with no clean packet, or at a desk where payroll, turnovers, and licenses all live in different places with different rules. That’s what we’re fixing.

Why Frameworks, Not Apps

Technology isn’t the problem. Vendors are full of good-intentioned people. The breakdown happens when compliance, cadence, and turnover aren’t built into the plan. Frameworks solve that gap. They connect law → workflow → staff → boards → residents. They make adoption possible in days, not years.

Proof and Pilots

In Hubbardston, we’ve already proven this. Even at 30% rollout, HubbCONNECT is handling DPW requests, turnovers, and licenses through unified workflows. Not perfect, but a real-world proof point that replacing gray with structure works.

Now, we’re moving forward with pilots. Three to five towns. Regional groups. Partners like CivicPlus, Microsoft 365, Laserfiche, Tyler, VADAR, and Polimorphic. We’re not replacing them — we’re making their deployments stick.

Flipping the Script

Innovation funding almost always flows uphill to larger cities and gateway communities. Small towns are told to wait. Public_Logic flips that script. The cracks start in small towns — that’s where the fixes should start too. That’s why 5–10% of proceeds fund the Diane & Fidèle Boudreau Public Innovation Legacy Fund, focused on communities under 25,000 people who’ve historically been left out.

Call to Action

This is Public_Logic. Frameworks that staff can run, boards can trust, and residents can see. Less gray. More clarity.

Smart Systems. Stronger Communities. People. Data. Systems.

Field_Note_001 — Pieces and FrameworksBreaking things down — and building them back stronger. 001  Aug 24, 2025

I’ve worked in and alongside several towns — from communities of four thousand to forty thousand — and spent time in both local and state legislative settings. At every scale, the story is the same: good people trying to do the right thing are often left fighting through unclear processes and disconnected systems. That’s where this starts. With people, not platforms.

This is our debut as Public_Logic. It isn’t about selling another municipal app. It’s about giving staff, boards, and residents the clarity they need to succeed. This note is the first in a series we’re calling Field_Notes, sharing what we’re building, why it matters, and how it connects back to the day-to-day reality of government work.

Pieces and Frameworks

Everything breaks into pieces. Career. Health. Even mistakes. What looks impossible becomes manageable once you stop treating it as one crushing weight and start seeing the smaller parts you can actually fix.

I’ve carried that lesson through hard times. Loops that came with an addictive personality. A cancer diagnosis that stopped everything in its tracks. Years of ethical uncertainty hanging over my career. None of it felt survivable at the time. What kept me moving was breaking things down and tackling what I could, one piece at a time.

That same approach carried over into municipal work. Local government is full of good people, but too often the structure sets them up to fail. Systems don’t connect. Data gets buried. Staff are forced into gray areas where the only option is to improvise.

The Gray Problem

Towns are drowning in gray — unclear processes, conflicting systems, buried data. Good people can’t succeed when they’re fighting the structure itself.

People. Data. Systems.

People work without clear expectations or consistent workflows. Data disappears into folder-within-folder chaos. Systems overlap and conflict, forcing staff to build risky workarounds. The gray compounds until nothing works efficiently.

Break it down, and you can build a framework.

One Pathway Forward

HubbCONNECT is proving this works. With about 30% rolled out in Hubbardston, we’re already seeing DPW requests, turnovers, and licensing flow through unified processes. Not perfect, but proof that replacing gray with structure works.

That’s Public_Logic: Verified Automation & Unified Logic for Towns. Not another app, but a framework that connects the pieces you already have. The underscore isn’t decoration — it’s the through-line tying it all together.

Underneath runs VAULT™ — an agnostic data framework layering compliance, workflow, and archive logic onto municipal operations. It’s the backbone that helps People, Data, and Systems work with each other instead of against each other.

People. Data. Systems.
