By Nathan R. Boudreau • nathan@publiclogic.org

Municipal-First by Design

Public_Logic didn’t start in a boardroom. It started in a life that needed clarity — and in town halls that needed the same thing. We are not selling another municipal app. We’re delivering a municipal operating framework so staff, boards, and residents can actually see the work and trust the process.

Frameworks connect law → workflow → staff → residents. When that connection is missing, adoption fails. When it’s present, implementation happens in days, not years.

Good People, Stalled Systems

Vendors aren’t the enemy; most are full of good-intentioned people. The problem isn’t the tools — it’s the fit. Municipal reality is compliance cadence, meeting cycles, capacity, and turnover. Public_Logic partners with CivicPlus, Microsoft 365, Laserfiche, Tyler, VADAR/MUNIS, and Polimorphic so deployments stick.

Lost in Transition

A grant-funded “modernization” launched and then was lost three times across transitions. Not malicious — just no framework. When adoption depends on a person, it dies with turnover. When adoption is a framework, it survives it.

ARCHIVE — The Base Model

Accountability, Records, Compliance, History, Integrity, Governance, Evidence (ARCHIVE) is the logic layer. Every module inherits its rules so actions are traceable, decisions are defensible, and continuity holds across boards and budgets.

The Operating System

On ARCHIVE sits the named suite of municipal frameworks — governance, finance, operations, regulation — each written in full and designed to be read in a public meeting. For the full map, see the Frameworks page.

Field_Note_001 — Pieces and Frameworks Breaking things down — and building them back stronger. 001  Aug 24, 2025 • ~5 min
By Nathan R. Boudreau • nathan@publiclogic.org

Opening

I’ve worked in and alongside towns from four thousand to forty thousand. The pattern is the same: good people fighting through unclear processes and disconnected systems. This starts with people, not platforms.

Pieces and Frameworks

Hard seasons taught me to break things down: loops from an addictive personality, a cancer diagnosis that stopped everything, years of ethical uncertainty. The way through was one piece at a time — and the same applies to municipal work.

The Gray Problem

Towns drown in gray — unclear processes, conflicting systems, buried data. People. Data. Systems. Break the gray into pieces, and you can build a framework.

One Pathway Forward

HubbCONNECT is proving it. About 30% rolled out: DPW requests, turnovers, and licensing flowing through unified processes. Not perfect, but proof that structure beats gray. That’s Public_Logic: Verified Automation & Unified Logic for Towns (VAULT™). The underscore is the through-line.
