About Public_Logic

We are practitioner-builders. We design municipal-first frameworks that make day-to-day work simpler, safer, and faster. Our practice comes from years inside town government—closing warrants, posting agendas, answering residents, and working inside Massachusetts General Law and local policy.

The difference is focus: frameworks, not software. Tools are useful; frameworks make them stick. We start with compliance, map the workflow staff actually live, and deliver adoption in days—not a fiscal year.

Public_Logic is platform-agnostic and municipal-first. We work with what you already have and make it work together. The result: clarity for staff, confidence for boards, and transparency for residents.

The Public_Logic Framework

Accountability, Records, Compliance, History, Integrity, Governance, Evidence (ARCHIVE)

ARCHIVE is the logic layer and standard base model. Every module inherits its rules for accountability, records, and compliance—so actions are traceable, decisions are defensible, and continuity survives staff and board transitions.

Principles

Compliance-first by design

Every framework starts with statutory requirements, audit readiness, and transparency. Compliance isn't an afterthought.

Platform-agnostic and practical

Work with what you have. Connect existing systems. Focus on workflow, not vendor lock-in.

Plain language, clear ownership

Names you can read in a public meeting. Documentation that survives transitions. Accountability that stands up to scrutiny.

Automate the boring stuff

Free up talented municipal staff to focus on residents and community building, not administrative busy work and system fighting.

Other Services

Beyond the operating framework, Public_Logic offers municipal-first services that help towns adopt faster, stay compliant, and keep work moving through transitions. For the full operating framework, see the Frameworks page.

  • Pilot Design & Adoption Sprints — pick 1–3 workflows, map to law, go live in days, not a fiscal year.
  • Compliance & Process Reviews — MGL, bylaws, policy mapping; gap analysis with practical fixes.
  • Documentation that Survives Turnover — plain-language SOPs, role handoffs, and board-facing checklists.
  • Board & Staff Training — Clerk, Finance, Health, Inspections, Planning; meeting-ready materials.
  • Budget Book Production — unified submissions, narratives, and public-ready budget books.
  • Capital Stewardship Workshops — lifecycle planning tied to debt schedules and replacement timing.
  • Vendor Alignment — make CivicPlus, Microsoft 365, Laserfiche, Tyler, and VADAR/MUNIS work together.
  • Transparency & Records — retention policy alignment, posting calendars, and public access improvements.
  • Grant & Funding Support — "ready-to-run" scopes, reporting cadence, and board update templates.
  • Regional & Shared Services — multi-town playbooks, cost-sharing structures, and governance models.
